Moo Goo Gai Pan II

Description

While you may see this dish frequently at American Chinese restaurants, it is actually
a true Cantonese dish. This is the Americanized version that will duplicate, what you're used
to eating for take-out. You can substitute sherry and soy sauce for the white wine to make
a version with brown sauce.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1/4 pound sliced fresh mushroom
  • 1/4 pound snow peas
  • 1 (8 ounce) can sliced water chestnuts, drained
  • 1/4 pound sliced bok choy
  • salt and black pepper to taste
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 tablespoon minced garlic
  • 1 tablespoon minced fresh ginger root
  • 3/4 cup skinless, boneless chicken breast - thinly sliced
  • 1 tea spoon white sugar
  • 1/4 cup chicken broth
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 2 tablespoon water

Steps

  1. Step
    2.

    Heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil in a wok or large skillet over high heat.
    Stir in the mushrooms, snow peas, water chestnuts, and bak choy; season to taste
    with salt and pepper. Cook and stir until vegetables are just tender, about 5 minutes.
    Remove the vegetables from the wok and wipe the wok clean

  2. Step
    3.

    Heat the remaining 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil in the wok. Stir in the garlic
    and ginger; cook a few seconds until the garlic begins to turn golden brown.
    Stir in the chicken and cook until the chicken is no longer pink, about minutes.
    Add the wine, sugar and chicken broth; bring to a boil. Dissolve the cornstarch in
    the water and stir into the simmering sauce. Once the sauce returns to a simmer, stir
    until thick and clean, about 30 seconds. Return the vegetables to the wok and toss until
    hot and coated with the sauce.
